About Us

PLATO was developed at Vanderbilt University in the Ritchie Lab.
PLATO was developed by Scott Dudek with help from the Ritchie Lab.

WASP and WASP Plotter were developed at Vanderbilt University in the Center for Human Genetics Research. The initial developement was intended to meet the needs of the International Multiple Sclerosis Genectics Consortium's Whole Genome Association screen recently published in The New England Journal of Medicine.
WASP was developed by Justin Giles with the help of David Sexton, Jacob McCauley PhD, Yuki Bradford, and Will Bush.

PLATO is currently being extended to more QC and analytical features and creating a graphical user interface.

For technical assistance or comments, please contact Justin Giles at: giles at chgr.mc.vanderbilt.edu.
